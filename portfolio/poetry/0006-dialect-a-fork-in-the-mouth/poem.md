# Dialect / A Fork in the Mouth

---

## I. GRANDMOTHER

| she said | she meant |
|---|---|
| wis at the gaitherin, the wan alang the watter | she was at the gathering place, the one along the river |
| afore the brigg wis biggit, afore the cooncil raxed its hands | before the bridge was built, before the council extended its reach |
| her mooth fu o cörní, auld words, guid words | her mouth full of longing, old words, good words |
| the kind nae body fae ootby wad ken | the kind nobody from outside would know |
| she'd sit by the fire an the word wad come oot like breath | she'd sit by the fire and the word would emerge like breathing |
| cörní, she'd say, an her een'd gang tae the windae | cörní, she'd say, and her eyes would go to the window |
| nocht there but the hills an the mairches she kent | nothing there but the hills and the borders she knew |
| an me on her knee, thinkin it wis the name o a place | and me on her knee, thinking it was the name of a place |
| I'd look fur the place on the map, the road bi the loch | I'd look for the place on the map, the road by the lake |
| cörní's no a place, she'd lauch, it's the — | cörní's not a place, she'd laugh, it's the — |
| an she'd trail off, her haund on ma heid, the fire crackin | and she'd trail off, her hand on my head, the fire crackling |
| ye'll ken it, hen, when ye feel it | you'll know it, girl, when you feel it |

## II. MOTHER

| she said | she meant |
|---|---|
| she never said it at the start, jist talked roond it | she never said it at the start, just talked around it |
| thon feelin, she'd say, ye ken, like — | that feeling, she'd say, you know, like — |
| she'd shape her mooth but naethin wid come oot | she'd shape her mouth but nothing would come out |
| coorie in, she'd say, a different wird, a safer yin | cuddle in, she'd say, a different word, a safer one |
| efter the schuil, efter the belt fur speakin wrong | after the school, after the belt for speaking wrong |
| she'd start in the auld vyce, then swalla it back | she'd start in the old voice, then swallow it back |
| her lips wid move but naethin — the radio coverin the soun | her lips would move but nothing — the radio covering the sound |
| whin I asked her whit granny meant, she'd jouk the question | when I asked her what granny meant, she'd dodge the question |
| it's jist a wird, she'd say, means naethin the noo | it's just a word, she'd say, means nothing now |
| an her haund on her chest, like she wis haudin in a beast | and her hand on her chest, like she was holding in a beast |
| she'd say it in her sleep sometimes, nearly, hauf-caucht | she'd say it in her sleep sometimes, nearly, half-caught |
| wi the radio on — stations she didnae hear — | with the radio on — stations she never heard — |
| the wird that didnae hae a hame ony mair | the word that didn't have a home anymore |
