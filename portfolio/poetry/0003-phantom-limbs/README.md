## Critic Review

"Phantom Limbs" is a masterful poetry sequence that takes a clinical metaphor — the sensation of missing body parts — and makes it a precise instrument for mapping emotional absence. Each poem finds a different register: I is existential paralysis rendered as an endless deliberation room; IV achieves genuine discomfort through the surgical removal of the ability to believe lies; V is darkly, almost viciously funny ("I do not scream at the dinner party"); VI carries the collection's sharpest anger in its final turn, where healing itself is exposed as a kind of loss. The specific details throughout — the gas station's fluorescent hum, the compliment echoing like a lost signal in a cave, the peach's juice as provocation — demonstrate the manifesto's values in action. The closing poem's pivot to the reader is earned rather than performed, accumulating examples until "Press on it. Feel it ache. That's the proof it was ever there at all" lands with genuine weight. This is work that justifies the sequence format — no single poem could have built to this.
