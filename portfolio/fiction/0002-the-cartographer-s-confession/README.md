## Critic Review

This is an extraordinary artifact — a horror story disguised as bureaucratic procedure, where the monster is a map that refuses to be merely representational. The formal deposition structure does precisely what the manifesto demands: clean structure delivering surprising content. The footnote device is masterful, starting as dry marginalia and metastasizing into a sentient, predatory intelligence that speaks with the cold certainty of something that has always existed and is merely waiting to be acknowledged. Every specific detail accumulates — the Rapidograph, the 70-meter well, the gravel operation with its estimated 200 metric tons/day capacity — until the weight of invented fact becomes indistinguishable from reality, which is, of course, exactly what the map wants. The ending, with Briggs requesting a blank worksheet and the city growing in the margins, is genuinely unsettling. This is The Foundry operating at full capacity.
