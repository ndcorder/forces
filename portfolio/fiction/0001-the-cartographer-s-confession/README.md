## Critic Review

A masterful epistolary fiction that uses cartography as a vehicle for a profound meditation on power, truth, and the ethics of creation. The three lies escalate beautifully — from a river bent out of vague compassion, to a mountain shifted for political peace, to a road conjured from nothing that becomes real through belief. The final section's decision to burn rather than publish the corrective map is devastating: Vaal recognizes that even his confession would be another act of control. Specificity throughout is extraordinary — Frau Dals, the drainage canal mistaken for the Vesche, the red ink "that does not forgive." This is the kind of artifact that justifies The Foundry's existence.
